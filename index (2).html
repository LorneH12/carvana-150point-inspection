Welcome to the Carvana 150-Point Inspection

Section One: The Carvana Standard

The Carvana Standard is our benchmark for making sure every vehicle is safe, clean, reconditioned, and ready for delivery. These expectations guide your day-to-day work and protect our customers on every drive.

Before a vehicle moves forward in the process, key safety systems must meet our requirements. If something is unsafe, the vehicle cannot be delivered.

  • Brakes
  • Tires
  • Steering
  • Suspension
  • Lights
  • Airbags & onboard diagnostics

Beyond safety items, we verify that major mechanical systems are working as expected. You help ensure vehicles perform the way customers expect on day one.

Vehicles are thoroughly detailed inside and out so the car that arrives in a customer's driveway feels truly "like new." Cleanliness includes interior surfaces, glass, upholstery, odor removal, and exterior shine.

A consistent appearance builds trust — customers should feel that their vehicle has been prepared with care and attention to detail, not just quickly washed.

All findings are documented accurately and shared with customers in the vehicle listing so there are no surprises. Good documentation protects customers, technicians, and the business.

Section Two: Flow in the Recon Center

How Vehicles Move Through the Recon Center

Every vehicle follows a defined path through the recon center — from arrival and check-in, to mechanical inspection, cosmetic repairs, detailing, and final quality checks. A reliable flow keeps vehicles moving, reduces bottlenecks, and protects the customer's promised delivery date.

As you watch the video, focus on where the vehicle is in the process, who owns each step, and what has to be true before the vehicle moves forward. Your job is one link in the chain — when each link is strong, the whole system works.

Three Things That Keep Flow Moving

  • Clear hand-offs: Every vehicle should have a clear "owner" at each step so it never sits waiting for someone to decide what's next.
  • Ready work: Parts, tools, work orders, and documentation are prepared before the vehicle reaches your bay whenever possible.
  • Signal and escalation: When something blocks the vehicle (parts, safety findings, missing info), the right people are notified quickly so the flow can be restored.

Section Three: Quality & Safety

At Carvana, Quality & Safety are non-negotiable. Every inspection, torque check, test drive, and verification protects the customer and the team members who worked on the vehicle.

Your attention to detail in this phase ensures that each vehicle leaving the recon center is not only ready for the road, but also documented clearly. When issues are found, they are fixed, documented, and — if needed — escalated.

  • Use the checklists: They exist to make sure no critical step is missed, even on busy days.
  • Own what you sign: When you mark an item as complete, you are saying "this meets the Carvana Standard."
  • Speak up for safety: If you see a concern — with a vehicle, a tool, or the environment — bring it up. Safety is everyone's job.
